Vietnamese Current Problems Traditional folk rhymes are becoming less familiar to younger generations in Vietnam. As digital entertainment grows, many children and young people no longer engage with these cultural materials in everyday life. This creates a risk that an important part of Vietnam’s intangible cultural heritage will gradually fade from cultural memory.
Solutions We aim to revive folk rhymes by transforming them into an interactive digital mystery game. By combining Vietnamese folk culture with AI technologies such as OCR and LLMs, we make traditional heritage more engaging, accessible, and relevant to the digital generation. This approach both preserves cultural values and connects them with modern innovation.
Duties Our duty is to preserve and promote Vietnamese traditional cultural values in ways that fit the modern era. We also have the responsibility to use AI carefully and critically, recognizing its limits and ensuring that human interpretation remains important, especially when dealing with cultural meaning and authenticity.

Brief Introduction

We are VinUniversity students majoring in Psychology in the College of Arts and Sciences, with a strong desire to contribute to our community and society. This project represents a small but meaningful step in our effort to preserve Vietnam’s intangible cultural heritage in the digital age.

About project

Folk Rhymes – The Cultural Identity of the Vietnamese Nation

The project is guided by a specific creative goal: “To build an interactive puzzle-solving experience based on the intersection of folk culture and AI technology.”

Central research question

How can OCR (Optical Character Recognition) and LLMs support detectives in decoding damaged texts, and when AI produces errors (hallucinations), how do these inaccuracies affect the investigative process?

Hypothesis

The digitization process is never 100% perfect. The gap between the “original handwritten text” and the “AI-interpreted version” becomes the source of narrative tension within the game.

Creative objective

To construct a “Digital Case File” experience, in which folk rhymes function as fragmented clues found at a crime scene. The player (acting as a detective) must use digitization tools to reconstruct torn handwritten notes and apply language analysis tools to uncover the logical connections between the rhymes and the case.
